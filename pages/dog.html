Local politician pets dog

Salt Lake City, reported by Alice Snacksalot

After a successful term of doing nothing, Sen. Steve Burgersworth announced his campaign for reelection by petting a dog in the park.

"The people of Utah love dogs, and I want them to know that I love dogs, too. I think it's important that we're on the same page with this," he said.

After the ceremony, a staffer from Sen. Burgersworth's campaign committee spoke with reporters about the next stops on the trail. Following today's events, he will visit the local homeless shelter to pet their dogs. Next week, Sen. Burgersworth will be in Provo, where he is expected to discuss the BYU Creamery's contribution to the obesity epidemic, followed by a quick trip to Orem to speak in a town hall meeting on the local shortage of quality sandwich shops.

Throughout his first term, Sen. Burgersworth became renowned for his ability to dodge any real work. During the last session of Congress, he sponsored no bills, participated on no committees of consequence, and frequently had to be woken up to cast his vote.

"The man is an absolute inspiration, and it's amazing what he's doing for this state," remarked Wally, a local software engineer. "I voted for him last time, and I'll vote for him again."

"There's not a person in Congress who doesn't respect Steve Burgersworth," reported Senate Majority Leader Elizabeth Gingersnap. "Steve has the rare gift to reach past partisan lines and encourage us all to do absolutely nothing."
