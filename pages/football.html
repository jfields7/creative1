Star quarterback quits football

Providence, Rhode Island, reported by Dan Smith

"He's phenomenal," said ESPN sportscaster Steve DiPasta.

"Kid's got a straight ticket to the NFL," reported Coach Rice from Tiny State University.

These are just a few of the reports surrounding football phenomenon Allen Flugelsnorg. The sophomore quarterback led TSU to a #3 finish in the College Playoff Rankings, narrowly losing to #2 University of Hickville in the semifinal game.

Now he's done. Flugelsnorg reported Wednesday that he is leaving the team to protect his health and focus on his degree in marketing.

When asked about his decision, he replied, "I just gotta do what's right for me, y'know? Anything can happen out there. Guys get hit all the time. I got sacked in the UH game and got a concussion. Made me rethink some things. I can't keep playing football forever, y'know? Something might happen, I end up a vegetable. Just didn't want it to happen, so I'm quitting the team to focus on the skills I can use for longer."

The TSU team doctor Allison Hurtsdonit offered her full endorsement for Flugelsnorg's choice, saying, "Allen's taken a lot of hits. He's a talented kid, but he's gotten a major head injury every season. You can't take too many of those before it starts to affect your day-to-day life."
